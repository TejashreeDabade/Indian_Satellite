Youthsat

YOUTHSAT is a joint Indo-Russian stellar and atmospheric satellite mission with the participation of students from Universities at graduate, post graduate and research scholar level. With a lift-off mass of 92 kg, Youthsat is a mini satellite and the second in the Indian Mini Satellite (IMS) series. Youthsat mission intends to investigate the relationship between solar variability and thermosphere-Ionosphere changes.

 

Lift-off Mass 92 kg
Orbit Period 101.35 min
Dimension 1020 (Pitch) x 604 (Roll) x 1340 (Yaw) mm3
Attitude and Orbit Control 3-axis body stabilised using Sun and Star Sensors, Miniature Magnetometer, Miniature Gyros, Micro Reaction Wheels and Magnetic Torquers
Power Solar Array generating 230 W, one 10.5 AH Li-ion battery
Mechanisms Paraffin Actuator based Solar Panel Hold Down and Release Mechanism
Launch date April 20, 2011

Launch Mass: 92 kg
Manufacturer: ISRO
Owner: ISRO
